### ==Na - adj: ==

- na-adjective can directly modify a noun following it by sticking 「な」 between the adjective and noun.

> - 静かな⼈。 Quiet person. 
> - きれいな⼈。 Pretty person.

We can use adj with particles like this:

>- 友達[[Particles#は (wa) - _Topic Marker_|は]]親切。 Friend is kind. 
>- 友達[[Particles#は (wa) - _Topic Marker_|は]]親切な⼈だ。 Friend is kind person.

And can be conjugated as noun

Present
>- ボブは⿂が好き[[State of beings#Negative state of being|だ]]。 Bob likes fish. 
>- ボブは⿂が好き[[State of beings#Negative state of being|じゃない]]。 Bob does not like fish. 

Past
>- ボブは⿂が好き[[State of beings|だった]]。 Bob liked fish. 
>- ボブは⿂が好き[[State of beings#Past state of being|じゃなかった]]。 Bob did not like fish.

### ==I - adj:==

- Unlike na-adjectives, we do not need to add 「な」 to directly modify a noun with an i-adjective.

> - All i-adjectives always end in the Hiragana character: 「い」

> - 嫌いな⾷べ物。 Hated food. 
> - おいしい⾷べ物。 Tasty food.

> ==Do NOT attach 「だ」 to i-adjectives.

Negative
First remove the trailing 「い」 from the i-adjective, and then attach 「くない」 

> Example: ⾼い → ⾼くない 

Past-tense: First remove the trailing 「い」 from the i-adjective or negative i-adjective and then attach 「かった」

Examples:

- ⾼いビル。 Tall building. 
- ⾼くないビル。 Not tall building. 
- ⾼かったビル。 Building that was tall. 
- ⾼くなかったビル。 Building that was not tall.
