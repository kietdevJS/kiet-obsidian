*To associate a noun with a noun. Particles are one or more Hiragana characters that attach to the end of a word to define the grammatical function of that word in the sentence*

## は (wa) - _Topic Marker_

> Introduces what the sentence is “about.”

- Sets the **topic** or **context**.
- Often translated like “as for…”
- Neutral, common in basic statements.

**Examples:**

- わたし**は**学生です。  
    _As for me, I’m a student._

- 日本語**は**おもしろいです。  
    _As for Japanese, it’s interesting._


---

## が (ga) - _Subject Marker / Identifier_

> Points to the specific thing that performs the action or the thing being identified.

- Used when identifying **who/what**.
- Also used when introducing **new information**.
- Can sound more **specific** or **contradicting** than は.


**Examples:**

- だれ**が**先生ですか。  
    _Who is the teacher?_
- ねこ**が**います。  
    _There is a cat._ (introducing new info)
- わたし**が**学生じゃないです。  
    _I am the one who is not a student._ (contrast)

---

## も (mo) - _“Also” / “Too” Marker_

> Replaces は or が to show addition.

- Means **also**, **too**, **as well**.
- Shows similarity or continuation.

**Examples:**

- わたし**も**学生です。  
    _I’m also a student._
    
- コーヒー**も**のみます。  
    _I drink coffee too._
    
- きのうも雨でした。  
    _It was rainy yesterday too._
